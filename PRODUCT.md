# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is a Chinese-speaking learner who starts with little AI semiconductor investing background but wants to reason like a serious industry investor. They are reading expert PDF materials that contain dense market language, company references, supply-chain logic, and investment judgment, and need a guided environment that turns those materials into reusable mental models.

## Product Purpose

This product is an interactive HTML knowledge base for learning AI infrastructure and semiconductor investing from zero to expert. Success means the user can move beyond repeating the source documents' conclusions and can independently analyze a semiconductor or AI infrastructure opportunity by identifying the industry layer, supply-demand constraint, competitive structure, cycle timing, valuation risk, and personal portfolio fit.

## Brand Personality

Calm, rigorous, and empowering. The experience should feel like a patient buy-side analyst desk: dense enough for real work, clear enough for a beginner to enter, and skeptical enough to avoid hype.

## Anti-references

It should not look like a marketing landing page, a generic AI summary dashboard, a slide dump, a ticker-pumping newsletter, or a decorative card grid. It should avoid shallow summaries, unsupported certainty, one-way exposition, and visual effects that distract from judgment training.

## Design Principles

1. Teach decisions, not vocabulary: every important concept should end in a usable rule or checklist.
2. Preserve the expert's reasoning chain: show assumptions, evidence, mechanism, implication, and failure conditions separately.
3. Make the learner work: include prompts, self-tests, scenario drills, and comparison tasks rather than only explanations.
4. Separate industry truth from market price: distinguish secular direction, cycle timing, valuation, and position sizing.
5. Treat live market claims as provisional: mark dated assumptions and encourage verification through filings, earnings calls, and primary sources.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible interactions. Motion should be restrained and respect reduced-motion preferences. The interface should support long Chinese reading sessions with comfortable line length, clear hierarchy, and no text overflow on mobile.
